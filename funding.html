About the NCV Graduates Entrepreneur Fund

In response to Kenyaâ€™s growing unemployment challenges, the NCV Graduates Entrepreneur Fund was developed to provide a pathway for young innovators to launch sustainable businesses. Recognizing that NCV graduates often lack access to financial backing or business mentorship, this initiative is designed to fill that gap.

Background

  • High youth unemployment in Kenya.
  • Lack of accessible capital for NCV graduates with business ideas.
  • Need to stimulate entrepreneurship in the Meru region and beyond.

Proposal

  • Microloans between $250 - $2,500 offered to eligible NCV graduates.
  • Applicants must provide a faculty recommendation and pass a basic financial accounting exam.
  • Each loan application must include a detailed business plan with goals and milestones.
  • Funding will be disbursed in stages based on milestone achievement.
  • Applicants and businesses must be located in the Meru region.
  • Loans carry competitive interest rates, equal to those offered to reliable commercial borrowers.
  • Program managed by NCV through a dedicated account; no regulatory oversight expected since no deposits are taken.
  • Office operations based at St. Francis Village.
  • Management will require one full-time administrator, laptop, phone, and occasional travel support.

Management Expectations

  • The program should be self-sustaining after 2 years.
  • Repaid loans will be recycled to fund new recipients.
  • Loan approvals based on risk, scalability, and clarity of goals.
  • Preference for high-growth or innovative businesses over low-value small shops.
  • Program Manager is expected to act as a coach, frequently visiting entrepreneurs on-site.
  • Loan disbursements go to recipient's commercial bank account.
  • Recipients should eventually graduate to commercial bank lending.
  • Partnerships with at least two local banks to help guide recipients.

Goals and Bonuses (Year 1)

  • Issue 25 loans to individuals or groups.
  • Target: 90% loan compliance rate.
  • 75% of recipients meeting or exceeding business objectives.
  • Program Manager receives a salary equivalent to a Saint Francis teacher.

Program Launch

Target launch year: 2026. NCV graduates are encouraged to start preparing for the accounting exam in the meantime.
